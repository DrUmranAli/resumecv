Leadership & Industrial Relations

Throughout my career, I have taken on significant leadership roles focused on enhancing academic quality, staff welfare, and institutional policy. A major part of this work has been my extensive involvement with the University and College Union (UCU).

UCU Trade Union Roles (2017-Present)

As Vice President, Branch President, and Equality Officer, my work has included:

  • Leading the negotiation and signing of the recognition agreement between UCU and the University of Salford (2017).
  • Co-chairing the Joint Academic Consultation Committee (JAC), focusing on teaching, learning, and research policies.
  • Leading on the formation and implementation of policies related to Workload, Research Strategy (REF CoP2021), Mental Health, and more.
  • Acting as Lead Union Representative for the UoS COVID Planning Group (2020-2022), enabling a safe transition to online teaching, which was noted as a model plan by the Department of Education.
  • Serving as REF2021 Code of Practice Lead Negotiator, securing a written letter of support from UCU for the university's CoP, one of the only HEIs in the UK to receive one.
  • Leading the Cost-of-Living Support Measures Initiative, which secured cost of living payments for all staff for two consecutive years and measures for discounted/free food on campus.

Institutional Leadership

  • Academic & Professional Workloads Institutional Review (2018-present): Led the introduction of a new workload system (2021) and currently co-lead the workload steering group.
  • UOS BAME Network Chair (2019-2021): Organized quarterly meetings to support institutional EMI staff and identify workplace EDI challenges.
  • School of Music, Media & Performance Equality & Diversity Co-Ordinator (2006-2010): Responsible for E&D policy review, planning & delivery for 2000 students/200 Staff.
